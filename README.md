## Project Overview

This project develops an Excel-based stress testing framework to evaluate the resilience of major Indian banks under adverse economic conditions.

Using publicly available financial data, the model simulates moderate and severe stress scenarios across key banking indicators and combines them into a composite **Banking Resilience Index (BRI)**.

The objective is to identify which banks remain resilient during periods of financial stress and understand the primary drivers of vulnerability.

---

## Banks Analyzed

* State Bank of India (SBI)
* Punjab National Bank (PNB)
* HDFC Bank
* ICICI Bank

---

## Methodology

The framework evaluates banks across five dimensions:

| Component        | Weight |
| ---------------- | ------ |
| Capital Strength | 30%    |
| Asset Quality    | 25%    |
| Profitability    | 20%    |
| Liquidity        | 15%    |
| Risk Management  | 10%    |

### Stress Scenarios

**Moderate Stress**

* GNPA Increase: +1%
* CAR Reduction: -0.5%
* NIM Reduction: -0.2%
* ROA Reduction: -15%
* ROE Reduction: -10%
* PCR Reduction: -5%
* LCR Reduction: -5%

**Severe Stress**

* GNPA Increase: +2.5%
* CAR Reduction: -1.5%
* NIM Reduction: -0.5%
* ROA Reduction: -40%
* ROE Reduction: -30%
* PCR Reduction: -15%
* LCR Reduction: -15%

---

## Key Findings

* HDFC Bank achieved the highest Banking Resilience Index (BRI).
* ICICI Bank demonstrated strong profitability and capital resilience.
* SBI remained structurally important but showed moderate vulnerability under stress.
* PNB exhibited the highest stress sensitivity due to weaker asset-quality indicators.
* Asset quality and profitability emerged as the most significant drivers of resilience.
